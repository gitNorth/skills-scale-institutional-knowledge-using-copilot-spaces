# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Engineers

### Role Summary
QA Engineers create and execute test plans, validate acceptance criteria, and report defects. They maintain quality gates and provide sign-off for releases, ensuring products meet defined quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Collaborate with Developers and Product Managers on feature acceptance and bug triage
- Design, automate, and execute test cases across unit, integration, and end-to-end testing
- Track defect trends and drive continuous quality improvements
- Define and maintain test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Participate in pre-release verification and smoke testing
- Document test results and provide quality metrics to the team

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high test coverage and automation standards
- Support rapid, confident releases

### Typical Interactions
- **With Developers:** Collaborate on testability, define test scenarios, triage defects, and validate fixes
- **With Product Managers:** Clarify acceptance criteria, sign off on feature readiness, and advise on quality trade-offs
- **With Project Managers:** Provide input to release go/no-go decisions and track quality blockers
- **With DevOps Engineers:** Coordinate smoke tests and production verification

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews and acceptance criteria sign-off
- Defect reports with reproduction steps
- QA metrics and release readiness checklists

---

## UX/UI Designers

### Role Summary
Designers ensure products meet user needs through research, prototyping, and validation. They partner with product and engineering teams to translate user insights into intuitive, accessible experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Participate in requirements gathering and ideation sessions
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Validate designs with stakeholders and user feedback
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met
- Provide design specifications and guidance to developers during implementation
- Iterate on designs based on user testing and feedback

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered, accessible product experiences
- Reduce rework through early validation
- Build shared understanding of user needs across the team

### Typical Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Collaborate on feature scoping, user research, and success metrics
- **With Developers:** Work during implementation to refine specs, address technical constraints, and validate pixel-perfect delivery
- **With Stakeholders:** Engage in feedback sessions and usability testing to refine designs
- **With Project Managers:** Advise on timeline impacts of design changes and iteration cycles

### Typical Communication
- Design specs and style guides
- Prototype walkthroughs and feedback sessions
- Usability testing reports and design iteration notes

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers support automation, infrastructure, and deployment activities for the team. They enable rapid, reliable releases and maintain system health and observability.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and improve CI/CD pipelines and environment configurations
- Support monitoring, alerting, incident response, and recovery
- Advise on automation, scalability, and reliability best practices
- Ensure secure and performant deployment processes
- Document runbooks and deployment procedures
- Participate in post-incident reviews and continuous improvement

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, repeatable deployments
- Minimize unplanned downtime and incident impact
- Provide visibility into system health and performance

### Typical Interactions
- **With Developers:** Collaborate on deployment readiness, troubleshoot pipeline issues, and advise on infrastructure needs
- **With Project Managers:** Coordinate release windows, communicate deployment status, and flag infrastructure risks
- **With QA Engineers:** Coordinate environment setup for testing and production smoke tests
- **With Product Managers:** Surface operational constraints that may affect feature rollout or scalability

### Typical Communication
- Deployment checklists and release coordination
- Infrastructure status reports and incident postmortems
- Automation improvements and best practice documentation

---

## Stakeholder / Liaison

### Role Summary
Liaisons represent key business, operational, or functional perspectives (e.g., customer support, sales, operations, security) that are critical to project success but may not be dedicated team members.

### Responsibilities
- Participate in project kickoff and milestone reviews
- Surface domain-specific risks, requirements, and constraints
- Facilitate feedback loops between their organization and the project team
- Provide sign-off at project gates and approval points as needed
- Communicate project progress and decisions to their respective groups

### Goals
- Ensure projects align with broader business and operational needs
- Reduce surprises and integration risks
- Enable smooth handoff to support, operations, or go-to-market teams

### Typical Interactions
- **With Project Managers:** Participate in planning, attend status updates, and escalate blockers
- **With Product Managers:** Provide domain expertise on requirements and success criteria
- **With All Roles:** Offer feedback, clarifications, and sign-off on deliverables relevant to their area

### Typical Communication
- Kickoff and milestone review meetings
- Feedback and approval checkpoints
- Domain-specific requirement documents

---

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When planning projects, ensure all relevant personas are identified and included in communications and decision gates.
- Use the interaction patterns to clarify handoffs and dependencies across roles.
